Teams

A driver and his team are nothing without their driving machines. The popular image of an F1 car conjures a sleek, squat body with protruding nose, wide open-wheel tires, a driver’s helmet, and dazzling liveries screaming at impossible speeds down a straightaway before slipping into a turn with mathematical precision.

Formula One is a history of bold ideas, great leaps in motorsport technology, baffling blunders, and heartbreaking failures. However, amidst the long line of engine manufacturers and aerodynamics designers, some cars rise above all others as beacons of engineering success. Some of the beauty of Formula One comes from the formula itself. What results from these rules and regulations are magnificent automobiles capable of taking drivers to the heights of motorsport glory.

McLaren MP4/4

  • Years Active: 1988
  • Designers: Steve Nichols, Gordon Murray
  • Engine: 1,494 cc Honda RA168-E, mid-engine, turbocharged 80° V6
  • Races: 16
  • Wins: 15
  • Poles: 15
  • Fastest Laps: 10
  • Notable Drivers: Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost
  • The MP4/4 was one of the most dominant cars in Formula One history. In the 1988 season, it won all but one race and claimed all but one pole position. Its impressive win percentage of 93.8% is still unmatched.

Williams FW14

  • Years Active: 1991-1992
  • Designers: Patrick Head, Adrian Newey
  • Engine: 3,493 cc Renault RS3C/RS4, mid-engine, naturally aspirated 67° V10
  • Races: 32
  • Wins: 17
  • Poles: 21
  • Fastest Laps: 19
  • Notable Drivers: Nigel Mansell, Riccardo Patrese
  • This car was ahead of its time. It featured many technological innovations that are no longer allowed in current formulas, including: semi-automatic transmission, active suspension, traction control, and antilock brakes. The FW14 delivered Nigel Mansell and Williams Championships in 1992.

Ferrari F2002

  • Years Active: 2002-2003
  • Designers: Ross Brawn, Rory Byrne
  • Engine: 3.0 L Ferrari Tipo 051/B/C, mid-engine, naturally aspirated 90° V10
  • Races: 19
  • Wins: 15
  • Poles: 11
  • Fastest Laps: 15
  • Notable Drivers: Michael Schumacher, Rubens Barrichello
  • The F2002 was the instrument of Michael Schumacher's impressive 2002 season. This Ferrari is a literally screaming fast machine, and serves as a symbol of the team's domination at the turn of the century.

Lotus 25

  • Years Active: 1962-1967
  • Designers: Colin Chapman
  • Engine: 1,496-1,499 cc Coventry Climax FWMV Mk.II-5, mid-engine, naturally aspirated 90° V8; 1,498 cc BRM P56, mid-engine, naturally aspirated 90° V8
  • Races: 49
  • Wins: 14
  • Podiums: 18
  • Poles: 14
  • Fastest Laps: 18
  • Notable Drivers: Jim Clark, Mike Hailwood
  • A revolutionary design, Colin Chapman's Lotus 25 was the first stressed monocoque chassis in Formula One. The mid-mounted muscular V8 engine is iconic of its era. The 25 was best utilized by Jim Clark, who took the machine to 14 grand prix victories.

Red Bull RB9

  • Years Active: 2013
  • Designers: Adrian Newey, Rob Marshall, Peter Prodromou
  • Engine: 2.4 L Renault RS27-2013, mid-engine, naturally aspirated 90° V6
  • Races: 19
  • Wins: 13
  • Poles: 11
  • Fastest Laps: 12
  • Notable Drivers: Sebastian Vettel, Mark Webber
  • Like a purple exclamation point, "Hungry Heidi" was the last car to win a championship in the naturally aspirated V8 era. It was also the car to carry Red Bull and Sebastian Vettel to their fourth straight Constructors' and Drivers' championships.

Maserati 250F

  • Years Active: 1954-1960
  • Designers: Giacchino Colombo, Valerio Colotti
  • Engine: 2,490 cc Maserati A6, front-engine, naturally aspirated straight-six
  • Races: 46
  • Wins: 8
  • Poles: 8
  • Fastest Laps: 10
  • Notable Drivers: Juan Manuel Fangio, Stirling Moss
  • The 250F was an icon of the first decade of front-engined Formula One racing. This Maserati was Juan Manuel Fangio's first Championship car.

Lotus 79

  • Years Active: 1978-1979
  • Designers: Colin Chapman, Peter Wright, Geoff Aldridge, Martin Ogilvie, Tony Rudd
  • Engine: 2,993 cc Ford-Cosworth DFV, mid-engine, naturally aspirated V8
  • Races: 26
  • Wins: 6
  • Poles: 10
  • Fastest Laps: 5
  • Notable Drivers: Mario Andretti, Ronnie Peterson
  • "Black Beauty" was yet another innovation from designer Colin Chapman. The 79 was the first F1 car to take full advantage of the then-new concept of ground effect aerodynamics. Mario Andretti drove the 79 to a dominant 1978 World Championship.

Mercedes F1 W05 Hybrid

  • Years Active: 2014
  • Designers: Aldo Costa, Paddy Lowe, Geoff Willis, Mike Elliott
  • Engine: 1.6 L Mercedes-Benz PU106A Hybrid, mid-engine, turbocharged 90° V6
  • Races: 19
  • Wins: 16
  • Poles: 18
  • Fastest Laps: 12
  • Notable Drivers: Lewis Hamilton, Nico Rosberg
  • Though not as stastistically successful as its later successor the W1 W07 Hybrid, the F1 W05 Hybrid was the first Mercedes car of the turbocharged hybrid era. Thus, it is something of an introductory statement for the success of Mercedes in the era.

McLaren M23

  • Years Active: 1973-1978
  • Designers: Gordon Coppuck
  • Engine: 2,993 cc Ford-Cosworth DFV, mid-engine, naturally aspirated 90° V8
  • Races: 43
  • Wins: 6
  • Poles: 14
  • Fastest Laps: 10
  • Notable Drivers: Denny Hulme, Emerson Fittipaldi, James Hunt
  • The M23 brought McLaren its first World Constructors' Championship in 1974 and remained competitive four seasons after its introduction. Its tall engine cover and its driver James Hunt are both iconic of the 1970s.

Ferrari 312T

  • Years Active: 1975-1980
  • Designers: Mauro Forghieri
  • Engine: 2,992 cc Ferrari 015, mid-engine, naturally aspirated flat-twelve
  • Races: 90
  • Wins: 27
  • Poles: 19
  • Fastest Laps: 25
  • Notable Drivers: Niki Lauda, Clay Regazzoni, Gilles Villeneuve, Jody Scheckter
  • The Ferrari 312T won four World Constructors' Championshps, three Drivers' Championships, and 27 races, making it the most successful car in Formula One history. Reliable, modular, and quick, the 312T is an emblem of one of Ferrari's many dominant eras.
